Grammar: Practice (Present Perfect Continuous, Phrasal verbs, Modal verbs, and Question tags)

A. Present Perfect Continuous
Rule: We use Present Perfect Continuous (have/has been + verb-ing) to talk about an action that started in the past and is still continuing or has just finished and has a result now.
Example: She has been cleaning the park for 2 hours.
1. Choose the correct form:
James ___ (clean) out a pond for two hours!
2. Make the sentence in Present Perfect Continuous:
"They / plant flowers / all day."
B. Phrasal Verbs (make up, make up with, make out)
Rule: Phrasal verbs are a verb + one or two particles (prepositions/adverbs) that often have a special meaning:
make up — invent, create (придумать);
make up with — become friends again after an argument (помириться);
make out — see, hear or understand with difficulty (разобрать, различить).
1. Choose the correct phrasal verb:
After their argument, they decided to ___ .
2. Translate: "Она не могла разобрать, что написано на доске."
C. Modal Verbs: have/has to, don’t/doesn’t have to
Rule: have to / has to means "necessary, you must do it" (нужно, должен).
don't/doesn't have to means "not necessary" (не обязательно, нет необходимости).
Example: She has to recycle cans. You don't have to read the book if you don't want to.
1. Choose the correct answer:
You ___ (don’t have to) recycle cans if you don’t want to, but it’s better for the planet!
2. Translate: "Ему нужно убрать пруд."
D. Question Tags
Rule: Question tags are short questions at the end of statements. They are used to confirm information. Use a positive statement + negative tag, or a negative statement + positive tag:
Example: She is cleaning the park, isn't she? You don't have to go, do you?
1. Complete the tag:
She collects rubbish, ___ ?
2. Choose the correct tag:
We have to recycle, ___ ?
